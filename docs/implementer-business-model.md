# Implementer Business Model

## Best Implementer Offer

Sell "Property OS Install" as a premium operational upgrade for small owners and boutique agencies.

The buyer should feel they are buying:

- fewer repeated renter questions
- a better first impression
- cleaner listing workflow
- owner-controlled AI drafts
- a professional renter portal
- a repeatable weekly operating rhythm
- an implementation readiness cockpit that shows what is live, manual, configured, or blocked

## Suggested Pricing Logic

Pricing depends on property count, media readiness, custom design, integrations, and ongoing operations.

| Offer | Typical Scope | Pricing Logic |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Starter Install | one property, sample styling, manual listings | fixed setup fee |
| Premium Install | custom copy, media pass, portal polish, owner training | higher setup fee |
| Agency Workspace | multiple owners, white-label docs, workflow training | setup plus license |
| Monthly Ops | FAQ updates, listings, weekly review, support triage | monthly retainer |
| Integration Sprint | database, email, calendar, dry-run channel adapter | scoped project |

## Margin Design

- Keep discovery and data cleanup paid; they are where most implementation variance lives.
- Productize repeatable setup with intake schemas, proof packets, and acceptance gates.
- Charge managed operations for a named cadence, response window, and artifact volume, not vague access to AI.
- Quote third-party hosting, messaging, storage, model, and channel costs separately or with a transparent allowance.
- Reserve custom channel adapters, migration, legal workflows, and portfolio permissions for scoped work.

Track delivery hours, support contacts, model/runtime cost, incident count, owner review time, self-service coverage, and retention by edition. Promote an offer only after it produces positive contribution margin and repeatable acceptance evidence.

## Sales Motion

1. Audit current rental page, listings, and support process.
2. Show the premium renter portal and owner dashboard.
3. Run a 45-minute owner intake.
4. Produce the first property page and listing drafts.
5. Review `/admin/implementation` with the owner so production gaps are explicit.
6. Sell monthly operations once repeated admin is visible.

## Proof Assets

- before/after listing copy
- self-service FAQ coverage
- inquiry response time reduction
- owner review checklist
- mobile portal screenshots
- manual publication checklist
- privacy scan and release gate evidence
- readiness score, blocked-action list, and MCP readiness snapshot

## Partner Rules

- Do not promise occupancy or rent increase.
- Do not offer legal, tax, accounting, or tenant-screening advice as part of the template.
- Do not connect live posting APIs until official access, dry-run payloads, owner approval, and rollback are proven.
- Keep client private data out of public template repos.
- Never sell the community safety baseline as a paid unlock.
- Use written acceptance criteria, controller/processor roles, subprocessors, retention, deletion, and incident responsibility for every production install.

# Product Editions And Economics

## Product Principle

One open substrate, three paid layers. Community users receive a complete sample, safety controls, schemas, agent and skill packs, local MCP, portal source, and verification commands. Revenue comes from reducing implementation risk and owning operational outcomes.

## Editions

| Edition | Included | Buyer | Commercial model |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Community | public-safe workspace, sample portal, Codex/Claude packs, MCP, schemas, tests, install proof | owner or builder proving fit | MIT, self-supported |
| Partner Install | private client workspace, approved data migration, branded portal, auth/database/notifications, preview QA, training, handoff | owner or boutique agency | fixed implementation fee |
| Managed Property Ops | weekly review, content and listing drafts, knowledge maintenance, triage oversight, measured service report | operator wanting less admin | monthly service agreement |
| Agency Platform | multi-client rollout standard, white-label system, enablement, adapter governance, portfolio reporting | real estate agency or implementation partner | setup, enablement, support, optional license for proprietary add-ons |

## Free/Paid Boundary

Free: core code, safety policy, blocked actions, local workflows, sample data, validation, authority tests, and deploy documentation.

Paid: client discovery, private data work, exact brand and media, hosting configuration, migrations, integrations, monitoring, incident ownership, training, ongoing operations, and service-level commitments.

## Unit Economics Ledger

Every install records:

- discovery, implementation, QA, training, and support hours
- third-party hosting, database, storage, messaging, and model cost
- gross revenue, contribution margin, and payback period
- owner weekly admin time before and after
- renter self-service coverage and escalation count
- installation defects, incidents, and change requests

Do not optimize model cost before measuring total delivery labor and support burden. The strongest margin lever is a reliable, evidence-backed install with less rework.

## Validation Ladder

1. Brother pilot proves useful data, owner cadence, renter answers, listing workflow, and urgent escalation.
2. Three external installs test activation time, willingness to pay, support load, and repeatability.
3. Five paid installs establish baseline margins and the highest-value managed operations.
4. Agency pilot validates portfolio permissions, white-label needs, support boundaries, and partner enablement.
5. Only then package proprietary adapters or a hosted SaaS tier around proven repeated demand.

## Claims Standard

Use measured language: setup completed, questions covered, drafts prepared, owner time observed, incidents acknowledged. Never claim higher rent, occupancy, legal compliance, response speed, or autonomous operation without attributable production evidence.
